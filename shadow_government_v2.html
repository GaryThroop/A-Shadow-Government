A Shadow Government
"Government of the people, by the people, and for the people"
The federal government's tools for self-correction are broken. But the United States is not just one government — it is hundreds of independent governments, fifty of them dual sovereigns with their own courts, prosecutors, and constitutions.

This site exists to organize the people who understand that, connect them across every issue that matters, and make effective action as easy as possible for everyone — whatever their level of involvement.
"Why are we losing when there are so many more of us? Because they're specialized and we're not. They fund people whose entire job is one thing: pack the courts, roll back climate rules, control the media. We try to fight all of it at once and burn out.

This site works differently. Pick your issue. Work with others on that issue. Let the rest of us handle the rest."

Governor, we are in a state of emergency.


The federal government is holding approximately 60,000 people in detention facilities while elected representatives who attempt to inspect them are being barred and arrested. Federal agents have killed American citizens on American streets and concealed their identities. The administration froze $259.5 million in Minnesota's Medicaid funding while over a million people depend on the program. Personal data from 300 million Americans was copied to an unsecured server. The president has called for federal takeover of state elections.


The president took the country to war with Iran without congressional authorization.


None of these are separate crises. They are connected, and the connections lead to the Epstein files — files that implicate the people driving every crisis on this list. The federal investigation is dead because the investigation leads to the people running the federal government. Your state's criminal code, your state's courts, your state's grand juries, and your state's pardon-proof jurisdiction are the answer the founders built for exactly this situation.


Please consider what threshold would merit you declaring a state of emergency. I believe that threshold has been crossed — many times over. We need [Your State] to use the broad, fast authority an emergency declaration provides to aggressively investigate and prosecute the crimes in those files, and to refer evidence to other states when it intersects with their criminal codes.


Twenty-four governors acting means twenty-four simultaneous investigations that no pardon can reach. It means the people who drove every crisis we're facing are finally playing defense instead of offense. It means the distraction loses its purpose the moment the thing it was designed to protect is under investigation.


I am asking you to pick up the strongest weapon available to you. We need you to act.


Respectfully,

[Your name]

[Your city, [Your State]]

Christopher Armitage · Existential Republic · June 24, 2026 · Original on Substack

There's something that can stop the United States from fully becoming a dictatorship, and it's being left unused.

The federal government is holding about 60,000 people in detention facilities, and elected representatives who try to inspect them are being barred and even arrested. Federal agents have killed American citizens on American streets and hidden their identities while doing it. The administration froze $259.5 million in Minnesota's Medicaid funding while more than a million people depend on the program. DOGE copied the personal data of 300 million Americans onto an unsecured server. The president has called for the federal government to take over state elections.

The president took the country to war with Iran without congressional authorization.

Governors have declared states of emergency for far less than any single one of these. Together they represent the largest sustained assault on constitutional governance in American history.

None of these are separate crises. The Epstein files are the web we're caught in. The people starting these wars and funding the authoritarian takeover are in those files. That's the key.

The federal investigation into Epstein's co-conspirators is dead. SDNY had an active investigation. DOJ headquarters ordered the files transferred out of SDNY, and the investigation stopped. The FBI closed it in July 2025 with a two-page memo claiming no evidence existed. Nearly fifty women gave federal prosecutors the names of roughly twenty co-conspirators. Every bit of it went nowhere.

So who stops this?

How does Congress stop him? They pass a law and he ignores it. How do the courts stop him? They issue rulings and there's no one left to enforce them. Inside the federal government, the tools are broken. Every single one of them.

But the United States is hundreds of independent governments, and fifty of them are dual sovereigns with their own constitutions, their own criminal codes, their own courts, their own prosecutors, their own police, their own prisons.

Every one of those fifty governors has the power to declare a state of emergency. That declaration unlocks broad, fast, unilateral authority. The founders designed the country this way because they knew this exact thing could happen.

Twenty-six governors won't ever do this — they're part of the same organization causing the crisis. That leaves twenty-four. Twenty-four is enough.

One governor launching this investigation is the law working as designed. Every additional governor is a force multiplier. Two and the administration's resources are split. Three and they're split again.

Is Trump going to arrest twenty-four governors for investigating child sex trafficking? Hell no. And if he tries, that makes the story bigger, not smaller.

Then the whole thing cascades. A line attorney who received the order to shut down the federal investigation sits in front of a state grand jury — facing state felony charges no pardon can reach — and names the person who gave the order.

You want to end the war? Investigate the files. You want to save elections? Investigate the files. You want to open the internment camps? Investigate the files. You want to end the dictatorship? Investigate the files. It all leads to the same place because it all comes from the same place.

I don't expect your governor to do this right now. The point of sending this is to get them thinking about it and to show them what their constituents want. Enough of those messages and a governor and their staff are thinking about it. Enough of that and they are talking about it. Thoughts become conversations, and conversations can become actions.

Please, contact your governor.

The social contract
Nobody asked us. We never engaged in the negotiations. We never agreed. It was legally assumed — like the tech contract nobody reads — that by existing in this jurisdiction you consented to all terms.
"The social contract was written by and for the powerful. We were handed it and told: this is what you agreed to. By continuing to exist in this jurisdiction you agree to all terms. It is long past time to have the negotiation we never had — about what we actually want from the society we share."
The discussion we never had
This working group exists to surface the fundamental questions: What do we actually owe each other? What should government do and not do? What rights are non-negotiable? What would the social contract look like if we got to write it ourselves?

This is not a rhetorical exercise. It's the philosophical foundation that makes every other issue on this site coherent. If we can't articulate what we're fighting FOR, we're just reacting to what we're fighting against.
How you want to help — every rung matters
No wrong place to be. No pressure to move up. Change your level any time.
My current level:
1
Stay informed no exposure
Read the daily news. Understand your issues. An informed citizen is the foundation — and harder to manipulate.
2
Share your voice privately anonymous
Respond to polls, rate representatives, submit anonymous research tips. Your data improves working group strategy.
3
Amplify low exposure
Share content, forward to friends, recommend this site. Movements grow through personal networks.
4
Communicate in writing your levellow risk
Sign a petition. Send a letter or email to a representative. Submit a public comment. Your name on record at very low personal risk. The governor letter above is a perfect level-4 action.
5
Make phone calls low exposure
Call your representative's office. Script provided. Two minutes. Staffers tally calls daily. Most people never do this — that's why it has outsized impact.
6
Show up locally low exposure
Town halls, county board meetings, school board hearings. You don't have to speak. Being a body in the room is noticed.
7
Organize locally medium commitment
Join or start a local chapter. Host a house meeting. Relationship-based organizing is the most durable kind.
8
Visible public action visible, protected
Rallies, marches, vigils, demonstrations. Fully protected by the First Amendment.
9
Sustained pressure campaigns sustained
Coordinated call blitzes, office visits, constituent delegations over weeks and months. Creates the sustained pressure that actually changes votes.
10
Civil disobedience accepts consequences
Nonviolent direct action. Accepts legal consequences. Requires training. Essential for some moments. Not for everyone.
11
Strike & economic withdrawal high commitment
Withhold your labor, your spending, your participation in systems that depend on your compliance. The most powerful nonviolent tool available to ordinary people.
